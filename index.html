Introduction

Harsh winter weather creates serious challenges for drivers in cold‑climate regions. Heavy snowfall, icy roads, and poor visibility often make driving dangerous and unpredictable. Many drivers experience situations where their cars become stuck in deep snow, causing frustration, delays, and sometimes accidents.

Most vehicles and road systems are not designed to handle sudden or extreme winter conditions. Drivers often struggle with clearing snow, face unexpected repair costs, or rely on roadside assistance to get moving again.

To better understand and address these problems, this project applies the Design Thinking process, a human‑centered approach that focuses on real user experiences and innovative solutions.

Target Market

The primary customers are drivers living in regions with harsh winters such as Canada, Norway, Finland, Russia, and the northern United States.

Car rental companies in Europe, especially in countries like Germany and Austria, are also key beneficiaries due to strict winter tire regulations.

Logistics and transportation companies in cold regions are another important market, as delays caused by snow can lead to significant financial losses.

In contrast, the UAE market is not suitable due to its desert climate and lack of snow.

Design Thinking Process

The project followed the Design Thinking stages: empathy, observation, problem definition, ideation, prototyping, and testing.

Empathy was developed through interviews with a daily driver in Canada who frequently experiences being stuck in snow. Observation involved analyzing real‑world cases and identifying common challenges such as snow heaviness, access difficulty, and time required to clear vehicles.

The core problem was defined as: How might we help drivers prevent or escape situations where snow and ice immobilize their cars?

Solutions Overview

Solution 1: Smart AI Snow Car

A vehicle engineered specifically for snowy environments, integrating AI, adaptive tires, heating systems, and automated snow‑handling tools.

  • AI snow control system
  • Intelligent tire pressure adjustment
  • Retractable needle traction system
  • Wheel heating system
  • Automated snow shovel

Solution 2: Heated Sidewalk Snow Mattress

A ground‑installed heating system with sensors and salt spray to prevent snow accumulation and ice.

Solution 3: Automatic Protective Car Shield

A stationary protective shell that covers the vehicle and prevents snow and ice buildup.

Most Sustainable Solution

The Smart AI Snow Car was selected as the most sustainable option due to its adaptive energy use, reduced reliance on salt, and ability to function anywhere rather than being location‑dependent.

Conclusion

Through Design Thinking, this project explored innovative solutions to winter driving challenges. By focusing on user experiences and testing multiple ideas, the Smart AI Snow Car emerged as a sustainable and technologically advanced solution.
